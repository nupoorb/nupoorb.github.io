Autonomous 4-wheeled Robot

Holonomic omni-wheel drive robot with Arduino control, onboard camera, and pneumatic throwing mechanism, navigating via floor lines to complete arena tasks.

Challenges/Solutions:
  • Overcame camera issues (lighting interference, image distortion, debris, damaged lines) through advanced image processing techniques for accurate line detection.
  • Mitigated inertia-related problems (wheel slippage, unexpected movement due to 20+ kg weight) by implementing PID control with encoders at individual wheel and system levels.
Remote Controlled 3-wheeled Robot

Engineered a versatile remote-controlled 3-wheeled robot with holonomic drive and pneumatic throwing mechanism, operated via Xbox 360 controller.

Challenges/Solutions:
  • Unsynchronized wheel motion due to motor inconsistencies. Implemented PID control with encoder feedback. Integrated IMU to correct unintentional rotational motion.
Remote Controlled 4-wheeled Robot

Engineered a holonomic 4-wheeled drive robot with a gripping mechanism, featuring advanced stability control for versatile operation.

Challenges/Solutions:
  • The robot's relatively high center of gravity compared to its base area posed a significant toppling risk during operation. To address this, a proportional control system was implemented to manage acceleration and deceleration, effectively minimizing toppling risk and ensuring stable operation across various terrains and tasks.
