Project Description


The goal of this project was to engineer a versatile quadrupedal robot capable of autonomous movement, obstacle traversal, and slope climbing. To achieve this, I employed a modular codebase centered around the principles of inverse kinematics, enabling precise control over each leg's movement. This approach allowed the robot to emulate a diverse range of animal gaits, optimizing its performance for different scenarios.
PID Control and Encoder Calibration:
To enhance stability and control, I integrated Proportional-Integral-Derivative (PID) controllers. These controllers, coupled with encoder feedback, enabled real-time adjustments and fine-tuning of the robot's movements. Calibrating the encoders ensured accuracy in leg positions and overall navigation.

Animal Gaits:
I conducted in-depth research into animal gaits that paved the way for a dynamic repertoire of movement patterns. By adapting different gaits to specific tasks, our robot demonstrated remarkable versatility, seamlessly transitioning between walking, climbing, and overcoming obstacles.
Inverse Kinematics for Precision Control:
The foundation of our robot's agility lies in the implementation of inverse kinematics. This mathematical approach empowered us to calculate the joint angles necessary for achieving a desired end-effector position, ensuring precise and fluid leg movements.
